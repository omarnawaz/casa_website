Co-Benefits of Climate Action

Anthropogenic emissions, especially those originating from fossil-fuel combustion play a critical role in influencing climate change. Reducing these emissions through targeted climate mitigation action can lead to substantial co-benefits, including improved air quality, enhanced public health, and mitigation of environmental injustices. I leverage adjoint source receptor relationships to quantify how the changes in emissions induced by climate chagne and climate action could affect atmospheric composition in the future. I am especially interested in characterizing how transboundary air pollution exchange will evolve across different climate futures.

Climate Co-Benefits from Chile's Nationally Determined Contributions

In Nawaz et al. (2023) we explore the co-benefits of climate action in the context of the Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) of Chile. We find that climate futures that emphasize carbon neutrality substantially reduce PM2.5 and NO2 concentrations in Santiago, Chile. By commiting to its NDCs Chile can improve public health in Santiago.
